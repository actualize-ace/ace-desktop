# Closing your day

`/eod` is the mirror of `/start`. Where the morning grounds you, the evening closes the loop. Truth capture at the end of the container.

ACE reads your git log, your session log, your daily note, your patterns. Then it drafts back to you what actually happened: what moved, what didn't, patterns it's noticing, anti-state signals, a reflection. One round of input from you, and everything gets written — daily note, practice tracker, execution log, pulse snapshot, sitrep for tomorrow.

The principle is simple. **Days that don't close leave residue.** Unfinished items compound. Patterns you didn't name keep running. Tomorrow starts from fog instead of ground.

`/eod` gives the day a container. What moved gets honored. What didn't move gets seen — not judged, just seen. What's true right now gets captured while it's still warm.

It's designed for end-of-day. You can run it earlier if you want a mid-day checkpoint, but the ritual wants the full arc before it reflects.
