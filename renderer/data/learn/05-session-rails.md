# Your session rails

Context is finite. Attention is finite. A session left to sprawl eats both. ACE gives you three rails so your chats stay bounded, your thinking stays clean, and your work actually closes.

**Your context bar.** Every session tracks the tokens moving through it — what you send, what ACE sends back, and what's cached for recall. The bar reads that in real time. Green means plenty of room. Gold is a breathing warning, a quiet nudge that you're deep in and should start thinking about where this lands. Red means wrap it up — summarize, decide, close. It's not a hard cap. It's a signal, the same way your body signals hunger before you fall over.

**Your session timer.** When you open a pane, you choose how long this session gets — 15, 30, 60, or 90 minutes. At 80% the timer goes warning. At 95% it goes critical. Then it expires. The point isn't to rush you. The point is to force closure before drift. A conversation with no end time becomes a conversation with no conclusion.

**Three sessions per pane, max.** You can't open a fourth. This is on purpose. Scatter is the enemy of coherence, and past three active threads your attention is already split across too many surfaces. Close one to start another.

None of this is restriction. It's scaffolding — the shape that lets you finish things cleanly instead of leaving a dozen half-open threads across your day.

*The rails hold so you don't have to.*
